Print quality: Headline numbers are extraordinary. FY26 revenue +84% to ₹26,537 Cr; PAT doubled to ₹3,884 Cr. Q4 revenue doubled YoY at ₹8,480 Cr. India's largest non-Chinese module manufacturer with 15 GW module + 5.4 GW cell installed capacity. Order book ₹53,000 Cr (~2× revenue) with pipeline exceeding 100 GW.

What broke the headline reaction (stock fell 8% intraday): (1) Cash flow weakened significantly — the FY26 jump in receivables and inventory consumed cash even as accounting profit doubled; (2) Q4 EBITDA margin held at 19.6% vs Q1FY26 peak of 25.4%, indicating sustained pricing pressure; (3) Total expenses rose to ₹21,898 Cr driven by raw material costs.

FY27 guidance: Operating EBITDA range ₹7,000-7,700 Cr (from CEO Jignesh Rathod). Implies ~50-65% EBITDA growth from FY26's ~₹4,640 Cr. Aggressive guide; if delivered, the stock is materially cheap. If missed, the multiple compresses sharply.

Revenue mix: Utility & IPP projects 34.7%, overseas 33%, retail/B2C 20.8% (rose 84% to ₹5,515 Cr), EPC 11.6%. B2C scale is genuinely differentiating — better margin profile and stickier customer relationships than peers.

US exposure: Waaree Solar Americas (subsidiary) won $176M order for 586 MW modules in May 2025; deliveries start FY27. The US push provides geographic diversification that Premier Energies lacks.

▼ Risks

BEAR CASE
01Cash flow weakened. CFO/EBITDA below 50% in FY26. Accounting profit not converting to cash.
02EBITDA margin compression. 25.4% (Q1FY26) → 19.6% (Q4FY26). ~600 bps in 9 months.
03India sector oversupply by FY28. Domestic capacity will overshoot demand 4×. Pricing pressure inevitable.
04Chinese spillover. Global module prices down 50%+ in 18 months. ALMM protection won't last forever.
05Raw material cost volatility. Polysilicon & wafer prices. Margin sensitivity high.
06Capacity ramp execution. Multiple greenfield projects simultaneously — cost overruns possible.
07US trade policy. Tariffs on India-origin solar could impact Texas plant's economics.
08Tech disruption. N-type TOPCon, HJT, perovskite tandems require fresh capex cycles every 3-5 years.

Rating: HOLD · Conditional ACCUMULATE on dips to ₹2,900–3,000.

Waaree is the strongest fundamental case in this 7-stock set after Bharti Airtel. India's #1 module maker by scale, largest non-Chinese globally, profit doubled in FY26, ₹53,000 Cr order book, FII anchor strongest in solar peer group, PEG ~0.5×. If you have to own one solar manufacturer, this is the one.

The reasons I'm not screaming BUY:
(1) Cash flow weakening — the gap between accounting profit and operating cash flow widened materially in FY26. This is what spooked the market on results day.
(2) EBITDA margins compressed 600 bps over 9 months. The Q4 print at 19.6% margin is the new floor, not an anomaly.
(3) FY27 EBITDA guide of ₹7,000-7,700 Cr is aggressive. If achieved, the stock is cheap at ₹3,215. If missed by 15%+, the multiple compresses sharply.
(4) Sector capacity overhang. India will have 4× surplus by FY28. Even the largest players take a margin hit.

Tactical playbook:
• Existing holders → HOLD. The franchise is real; near-term volatility is the cost.
• Fresh money → ACCUMULATE in tranches at ₹2,900–3,000 (~7-10% below CMP) and ₹2,500–2,600 (~17-22% below). Both zones have technical support.
• Trigger for upgrade to BUY → one clean quarter showing (i) EBITDA margin stable or up vs Q4FY26's 19.6%, (ii) CFO/EBITDA back above 60%, (iii) FY27 guide on-track at first half.
• Hard stop ₹2,400 (below 200-DMA + sector overhang invalidation level).

Position cap: 3–4% of equity book. Best solar exposure for someone with 3-year horizon and tolerance for ±30% drawdowns. Pair with a cash-flow heavy compounder (like Bharti Airtel) to balance the cyclicality.
