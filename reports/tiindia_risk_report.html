Print quality: Tale of two narratives. Top-line momentum was stellar — record quarterly revenue of ₹6,215 Cr, up 20.68% YoY, driven by Engineering and Mobility segments. Operating margins held at 12.1%, indicating real underlying strength.

What broke the headline: An unusually elevated tax rate of 53.4% in Q4 (vs ~25-28% normal) crushed reported PAT to ₹85 Cr from ₹279 Cr the prior quarter. This appears to be a one-off tax provisioning anomaly, not a deterioration in operations.

Strategic moves: (1) Acquired Tivolt EV (100% stake, ₹30 Cr) on Apr 1, 2026 — consolidating EV manufacturing under TICMPL; (2) Completed acquisition of Orange Koi Private Ltd on Apr 7. TICMPL (TI Clean Mobility) remains in heavy investment phase — near-term margin drag, multi-year option value.

Institutional dynamics: FII selling continues (count down from 600+ to ~510). Domestic MFs absorbing supply (10.32% → 14.69% in 12 months). Notable divergence in conviction.

Bottom Line

FINAL CALL

Rating: HOLD · Wait for entry below ₹2,600 · Accumulate ₹2,400–2,600.

TIINDIA is genuinely a quality compounder — Murugappa group, multi-segment moat, record-high quarterly revenue, strong balance sheet, dividend-paying. The Q4 PAT miss is tax-driven, not operational. So the business is fine.

The issue is the multiple. At ~70× P/E for a primarily auto-anc business, the stock is pricing in the EV transition succeeding flawlessly — which it might not, or might take longer than expected. The 43% drawdown from ₹5,200 reflects that re-rating process is ongoing.

What I'm pushing back on: The bull case TPs of ₹3,500–3,650 assume TICMPL monetisation in 2-3 years AND tax normalisation AND continued auto cycle strength. That's three things that need to go right. ICICI's HOLD at ₹3,100 is the more honest call — near-term reward of +5% is not worth the downside risk.
