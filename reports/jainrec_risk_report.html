Print quality: Headline revenue growth was strong (+48% YoY), but the bottom-line missed Street estimates significantly. The market reaction tells the story — stock fell 18.5% on Day 1, another 15% on Day 2.

What broke: (1) Working capital cycle blew out from 45-50 days pre-IPO to ~90 days, indicating receivables stretch or inventory buildup; (2) Copper EBITDA/ton fell from ₹51,000 to ₹42,000 — a ~18% decline in unit economics; (3) Q4 PAT growth (+22%) lagged revenue growth (+48%) significantly.

Management commentary: First copper furnace commissioned. Plastic recycling plant (₹15 Cr) coming Q3FY27. International Conveyors acquired 87,105 shares on May 11 (₹5 Cr). MOSL cut FY27/FY28 EPS by 15%/16% but maintained BUY with TP ₹560 (27× FY28 EPS).

Thesis · Catalysts vs Risks

DUAL COLUMN

▲ Catalysts

BULL CASE
01Structural industry tailwind. India non-ferrous recycling CAGR 13-18% to FY30. Govt push on import substitution.
02First copper furnace operational. Capacity expansion in highest-margin segment. FY27 ramp visibility.
03High export mix (60%+). Marquee clients: Vedanta-Sterlite, Luminous, Mitsubishi. Lead brand LME-registered.
04Promoter alignment. 73.6% promoter holding signals long-term commitment (though lock-in expires Sep 2026).
05IPO money deployed. Debt paydown reduces leverage from 1.4× to 0.85× D/E.
06Forensic-clean balance sheet. No related party concerns, no governance flags at IPO.

▼ Risks

BEAR CASE
01Working capital cycle doubling. 45 to 90 days in 6 months. Cash conversion deteriorating sharply.
02EBITDA/ton compression. Copper unit economics down 18%. Volume-led growth, not pricing power.
03Lock-in expiry Sep 2026. Promoter and pre-IPO investor lock-ins will release supply. Overhang risk.
04Limited public track record. Only 4 quarters as listed. No history of weathering metal cycles.
05Commodity exposure. Copper, lead, aluminium prices drive margin — LME volatility = quarterly earnings volatility.
06FII confidence low. 3% FII holding for a ₹13,500 Cr company is exceptionally weak. Institutional anchor thin.
07Falling-knife dynamics. 33% in 2 days post-results suggests information asymmetry or programmatic selling.

Rating: AVOID / WAIT. Entry zone ₹250–280 post lock-in expiry (Sep 2026).

The bull case (copper recycling tailwind, capacity expansion, debt-light balance sheet post-IPO) is real but already priced in twice over at current levels. The bear case (working capital blow-up, EBITDA/ton compression, lock-in supply overhang) is what the market just woke up to — and the 33% two-day crash suggests there's more selling to come once the lock-in releases.

What I'm pushing back on: MOSL's ₹560 TP rests on 27× FY28 EPS — that's still premium to peers and bakes in margin recovery that the recent quarters do not show. If EBITDA/ton continues falling and working capital doesn't normalise by Q2FY27, MOSL will cut again.
