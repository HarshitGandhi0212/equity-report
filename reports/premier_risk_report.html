Print quality: Headline numbers are excellent — record quarterly profit (₹457 Cr, +64% YoY), record revenue (₹2,230 Cr, +37.6% YoY), full-year PAT up 75%. Solar module production climbed 37% YoY to 918 MW in Q4; cell production surged 59% to 722 MW. Order book stands at ₹14,010 Cr — ~6.4× FY26 revenue. New Q4 wins of ₹2,577 Cr (1,600 MW) for FY27-28.

What deserves attention: EBITDA margin compressed 350 bps YoY in Q4 (35% → 32%). This is the first quarter margin signal that competitive pressure is real. Bear thesis on solar oversupply is being validated incrementally.

The big move: Board approved fundraising up to ₹5,000 Cr via QIP on May 15. This is meaningful for a ₹44,000 Cr Mcap company — ~11% potential equity expansion. Pricing and timing not yet disclosed. New Company Secretary effective May 16, 2026.

Capacity context: Current 3.6 GW cell + 5.4 GW module capacity, expanding aggressively. India sector building 50+ GW of new module capacity by FY28; sector capacity will exceed domestic demand by FY27.

Thesis · Catalysts vs Risks

BULL vs BEAR

▲ Catalysts

BULL CASE
01Integrated cell + module. Better margins than module-only peers (Waaree); end-to-end value capture.
02Order book ₹14,010 Cr. 6.4× FY26 revenue. Multi-year revenue visibility into FY28.
03India PLI & ALMM tailwind. Government push for domestic manufacturing protects pricing; imports disadvantaged.
04Capacity scale-up to 10+ GW. First-mover advantage in Indian integrated solar — hard to replicate at scale.
05ROE 26% (pre-QIP). Genuine high-quality returns; superior capital efficiency vs peers.
06India solar TAM expanding. 500 GW renewable target by 2030 = 280 GW solar = 25 GW/year module demand.
07PEG ~0.8×. Most reasonable PEG among solar peers despite the premium absolute multiple.

▼ Risks

BEAR CASE
01₹5,000 Cr QIP dilution. ~11% equity expansion potential. Mechanical EPS and ROE compression.
02Q4 EBITDA margin −350 bps YoY. First sign of competitive pressure. Could accelerate as new capacity floods market.
03Chinese oversupply spillover. Global module prices down 50%+ in 18 months. Indian ALMM protection won't last forever.
04India sector capacity glut by FY28. Domestic module capacity expanding faster than demand — structural pricing risk.
05Concentration risk. Top 5 customers contribute majority of order book. Loss/delay of major IPP orders hits revenue.
06Stock not participating. −5% over 1 year despite 75% PAT growth = market scepticism of sustainability.
07Tech disruption. N-type TOPCon and HJT cells require fresh capex; current PERC capacity could face obsolescence.

Rating: HOLD · Await QIP pricing before committing fresh money.

Premier Energies has the best fundamentals in my solar peer comparison — integrated cell + module, ROE 26%, debt-light, ₹14,010 Cr order book, PEG ~0.8×. Q4 PAT of ₹457 Cr was a record. None of this is in dispute.

What I'm cautious on: Three things landed in May 2026 that change the math:
(1) Q4 EBITDA margin compressed 350 bps YoY — first concrete evidence of pricing pressure.
(2) ₹5,000 Cr QIP authorised — up to 11% equity dilution, terms unknown.
(3) India sector capacity will overshoot demand by ~4× by FY28 — structural pricing risk approaches.

Why the stock is flat for a year despite 75% PAT growth: The market has been pricing in dilution and margin compression even as headlines kept beating. That divergence is the most honest piece of information in this story.

Tactical playbook:
• Existing holders → HOLD. Q4 print justified holding.
• Fresh money → WAIT for the QIP. If priced at ₹800–850 (15–18% discount), the post-QIP entry on the open market becomes attractive at ₹900–950.
• Aggressive add → only after one more clean quarter (Q1FY27, due Aug-2026) confirms margins haven't broken further.
• Hard stop ₹760 (52W low). Below that, the sector overhang has clearly won.

Position cap: 2–3% of equity book. This is a thematic high-beta exposure, not a core compounder yet. Treat as such.
